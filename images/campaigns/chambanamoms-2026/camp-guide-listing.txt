ILLINOIS MAKERLAB — SUMMER 2026 CAMPS
======================================

Camp Guide Listing for ChambanaMoms.com
----------------------------------------

The Illinois MakerLab at the Gies College of Business (UIUC) offers five hands-on STEM summer camps for kids ages 10+. Campers work with 3D printers, AI tools, and real robots in a university maker space — taking home everything they create.

CAMPS OFFERED:

- Minecraft + 3D Printing (Ages 10+) — Build worlds in Minecraft and 3D print your creations. Our flagship camp, offered across 8 weeks.

- Adventures in 3D Modeling (Ages 10-17) — Learn professional design with Fusion 360 and 3D print your original models.

- Generative AI + 3D Printing (Ages 12+) — Use AI tools to generate unique designs and bring them to life with 3D printing.

- Build Your Own Robot Arm (Ages 12+) — NEW! 3D print a robot shell, assemble it with real motors, and train it to pick and place objects using AI. Only 5 spots per session.

- AI Robotics with Reachy Mini (Ages 12+) — NEW! Program a desktop robot that sees, listens, and talks back using Python and AI. Only 5 spots per session.

DETAILS:
- Dates: June 1 – July 31, 2026 (no camp week of June 29 – July 3)
- Schedule: Mon–Fri, 3 hrs/day (Morning 9am–12pm or Afternoon 1pm–4pm)
- Pricing: $250/camp ($225 early bird through March 15)
- Location: BIF Room 3030, University of Illinois, Champaign
- All computers and materials provided
- Add-ons: Lunch hour supervision $10/day, late pickup at 5pm $10/day
